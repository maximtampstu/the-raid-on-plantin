Hi, i am Christoffel Plantin and i was born in 1520, at this moment I am no longer with you. But have had a wonderful life; Curious about where I've been, how I started my printing business and how I got into trouble? Let's relive my life together and find out.

Dive in

My Beginning

I wake up, my father Jean Plantin is already up and in the kitchen, he tells me that I got a letter from my brother.

By brother he means Pierre Porret We lived together for a while and he really became my very best friend, in other words my brother.

I open the letter with a knife, the letter is about the plague epidemic around Tours. I remember that my mother is staying there. I immediately read on tense, word after word until I read “Ta mère est, par malheur, trépassée de la peste” (your mother has unfortunately died of the plague). I immediately start crying and look at my father, he already knew, he walks up to me and hugs me.

6 years later

Me and my father

My father often has to travel around for work. We have been in Paris for a while, he steps up to me and gives me a sum of money. He tells me he doesn't want to drag me everywhere anymore and says

“You should live on your own”

He does tell me that he will come back to take care of me again. But will he? I immediately start looking for a job. I offer myself in different places with little success. Many maîtres (employers) tell me that I must have done a study first or they won't hire me. So I don't stick to my bones and enroll in a study.

I am a few months far into my studies and receive an unknown letter from the courrier (mailman).

Normandy

This news I did not expect. I already have a lot of stress for financial reasons because I live in Paris and now this. Now a few days later I am quiting my studies and moving to Normandie where everything is much cheaper. I now have to find a new study, teacher is high on my list but after searching for a while I found nothing finacially feasible.

I continue thinking, I have quite an interest in books which is why I wanted to become a teacher. It comes to mind,

My Printing Empire

I have been working for several years now and have gotten the idea to start a printing business myself. I heard somewhere last week that Antwerp is in a boom period that is apparently somewhere in Belgium I am trying to get more info

and find a corner house in Antwerp where I can start. Me and my wife take the big step and move to Antwerp. After a short time we get the studio next to the corner house donated so we can properly start the printing business.

Problems

I do now bump into a problem the monnaie (money) is up. So I go to my wife to brainstorm together. Soon we bump into the idea of a store. The idea is to sell books or even separate sheets here. We start making the store.

But we are told by the Spanish king that there can be no Calvinist books because they are against Catholic doctrine and threaten the unity of the faith. For this the king says we have to make a list, after the list is finished I print it and walk to the store to hang it there. Some of the books we are not allowed to sell are my own books so I throw them on the fire pile.

With the money from the store I can now build a decent living part of the printing shop for my family. And eventually houses for my future children.

Game

Néerlandais

Now there is another problem, the language. everyone here speaks néerlandais (dutch) but I don't. I have trouble communicating with my employees I have been taking Dutch lessons for several months but with little success. My Dutch teacher has now given me the task of writing down all the words that I often need so that I can be sure to know the translation of them.

Weeks later

Now a few weeks on I have been using this a lot my employees love it and I see more smiles on their faces now. Now I am thinking that I can use this for all words and have this printed to publish.

Here was born the first dictionary that you know to this day

Now I can communicate with my workmen but not with the courriers (mailmen), these people often can't read well if at all so I start making a logo so the courrier (mailman) knows if he is at the right address or not.

My logo, the golden compass with the motto 'Labore et Constantia', represents precision, hard work, and balance between knowledge and faith.

Daughters

Spelling Correctors

This is very important because many spelling mistakes come up. Often a capital letter is forgotten or letters are missing in the words. I pay close attention to the qualities of my daughters.

New job

When they are 12, I remove them as spelling correctors and give them a task that better suits their qualities. Now my second daughter Martina Plantin is turning 12 I have been watching her qualities lately she has developed a real passion for linen. So I no longer let her be a spell corrector but let her make linen. We are now selling these linens in the store. She comes running up to me and shouts

“Dad, dad I have an idea, what if we hire seamstress to make clothes out of the linen to sell too?”
